CHARLES DARWIN | Voyage of the Beagle

  • Rio De Janeiro

Rio De Janeiro

Arrival: 1832 February

"A few days after our arrival I became acquainted with an Englishman who was going to visit his estate, situated rather more than a hundred miles from the capital, to the northward of Cape Frio. I gladly accepted his kind offer of allowing me to accompany him."

The day was powerfully hot, and as we passed through the woods, everything was motionless, excepting the large and brilliant butterflies, which lazily fluttered about.

Leaving the coast for a time, we again entered the forest. The trees were very lofty, and remarkable, compared with those of Europe, from the whiteness of their trunks.

Fun Fact:

Charles Darwin is best known for his work as a naturalist, developing a theory of evolution to explain biological change.
